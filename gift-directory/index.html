WAYFARE'S DIFFERENCE MAKERS GIFT DIRECTORY

Giving gifts is one of the best parts of life. Giving gifts that make a difference and are produced responsibly is even better. Here is a list we've compiled of companies that source and make their goods ethically. By purchasing many of these items, you are also making a difference in the lives of some of the most vulnerable people in our world. Feel free to contact us if you'd like to to see something added to this list. Happiest shopping!

RESEARCH RESOURCES

  • Free2Work (www.freetowork.org)
    Learn how your favorite brands relate to trafficking and other labor abuses, view lists of companies by ratings.
  • Better World Shopper (www.betterworldshopper.org)
    Rates over 1000 companies based on human rights, the environment, animal protection, community involvement, and social justice.

CLOTHING

  • Krochet Kids (www.krochetkids.com)
    Headwear, apparel, and accessories that provide jobs for women in Uganda and Peru.
  • Threads for Thought (www.threadsforthought.com)
    Fair trade and environmentally friendly clothing company. Sold locally at Seed Peoples Market and Whole Foods.
  • Sevenly (www.sevenly.org)
    For each shirt you buy, $7 is donated to a featured charity.
  • Everlane (www.everlane.com)
    Fair trade apparel company sourcing their products ethically from around the world.
  • Povertees (www.povertees.com)
    LA based T-shirt company that aids willing homeless individuals out of the cycle of poverty.
  • Patagonia (www.patagonia.com)
    Environmentally and socially responsible outdoor clothing company.
  • Alternative Apparel (www.alternativeapparel.com)
    Modern basics made of sustainable, environmentally friendly materials.
  • Smart Wool (www.smartwool.com)
    Sustainable outdoor apparel that has low environmental impact and is made in the USA.
  • Raven + Lily (www.ravenandlily.com)
    Women's lifestyle brand that empowers women around the world with employment.
  • Pact Underwear (www.wearpact.com)
    Super soft, organic, cotton underwear, socks, and tees.
  • I am a Star (www.iamastar.org)
    Clothing company with a mission to fight the food crisis in Somalia.
